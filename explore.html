BASIC COMPUTER PARTS & THEIR FUNCTIONS

Motherboard

-a motherboard is the main circuit board in a computer system. It connects all of the internal components, like the memory, processor, graphics card and other hardware. It also provides power to each component and allows them to communicate with each other.



Processor

-the part of the computer that interprets data, executes Instructions, and solves arithmetic problems; the main Information processor or "brain" of the computer.



Random Access Memory (RAM)

-also known as read and write memory, is a type of transient internal memory that the user can access and modify.




Hard disk drive

-also known as read and write memory, is a type of transient internal memory that the user can access and modify.




Optical Drives

-CD or DVD drive, often found on the system units front. In addition to being able to write (record) data onto CDs, many CD drives also use lasers to read (retrieve) data from CDs. CD drives can also be used to play music files on a computer.



Floppy disk drive

-floppy disk drives store information on floppy disks, also called floppies or diskettes.



Integrated Drive Electronic (IDE) Cable

-it is a typical kind of connection for a PCs storage devices. In general, it refers to the many connections and ports that are utilized to link the motherboard, certain hard drives, and optical drives to one another.



Serial ATA (SATA)

-is a computer bus interface that connects host bus adapters to mass storage devices such as hard disk drives and optical drives.



Floppy cable

-a ribbon cable used in PCs that enables the connection of one or more floppy disk drives to a computer.



Video card

-the graphics component is handled by the video card. It processes almost everything that is shown on the monitor, including pictures, videos, and 3D games.



Sound card

- a sound card is necessary if you want sound output from your computer. Sound cards can be either separate components or directly integrated on the motherboard, and usually every PC has them.



Modem

- a device that converts signals from one form to a form compatible with another kind of equipment to transmit data between computers, generally over phone lines.



USB Card

- a sound card is necessary if you want sound output from your computer. Sound cards can be either separate components or directly integrated on the motherboard, and usually every PC has them.



LAN card

-a network card is a component that is used to connect to the Internet or create a link between several computers. Similar to sound cards, network cards can be purchased as separate parts or integrated into the mainboard.



Power supply

-the power supply unit converts AC power from your wall outlet to DC power, usable by the computer internals, and controls power saving.



Automatic Voltage Regulator (AVR)

-a device that maintains the terminal voltage of a generator or other voltage source within required limits despite variations in input voltage or load.



Power cord

-a card to conduct power. Desktop computers typically use a universal power cord for power, while laptops use power adapters, often made to the specifications of the manufacture.



Keyboard

-The tool used to enter data is the keyboard. An older serial connection or a more modern USB connector can be used to connect a keyboard to a computer. Keyboards are useful devices for more than just data entry.In addition to video controls, they may also have controls for audio and video, like a DVD player.
